REAL SCENARIO

The Harumanis (*Mangifera indica* MA 224) Challenge

In Perlis, Malaysia, the premium Harumanis mango demands precision cultivation. To achieve export quality, agrotechnologists must optimize fertilization (especially Nitrogen and Potassium) and determine the exact timing for protective bagging (typically done to prevent Fruit Fly Bactrocera dorsalis attacks).

Fruit weight development follows a classical sigmoidal (logistic) curve. The absolute growth rate is the first derivative, \( f'(x) \). By analyzing where \( f'(x) \) peaks (the point of inflection), agrotechnologists can pinpoint the exact day of maximum physiological demand, optimizing irrigation cycles and nutrient delivery.

CALCULUS MODEL

The Logistic Growth Function

Weight Function \( f(x) \)
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
Derivative \( f'(x) \) (Growth Rate)
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$

Parameters Modelled:
Max Capacity (\(L\)) = \(400\text{g}\) | Growth Rate Scaler (\(k\)) = \(0.08\) | Inflection point (\(x_0\)) = \(60\text{ DAA}\)

How to apply these Derivatives to your Agrotech Curriculum:

1 Inflection Point Finder

Identify the maximum point on the \(f'(x)\) curve. On Day 60, the growth rate hits its absolute peak at \(8.00\) grams per day. This marks the transition point (inflection point of \(f(x)\)) from accelerating weight gain to decelerating development.

2 Resource Allocation

By aligning high growth rates \(f'(x) \ge 5.0\) (spanning Day 40 to 80 DAA) to your irrigation models, you can conserve massive quantities of water during early physiological phases and target irrigation perfectly when the fruit requires cell-swelling.

3 Harvest Window Predictor

When the first derivative \(f'(x)\) approaches \(0.0\) (Day 100+), the mango is entering commercial maturity. Starch is converting to sugars, rendering the flesh sweet, aromatic, and ready for selective hand-picking.
